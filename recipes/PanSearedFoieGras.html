Pan Seared Foie Gras

An elegant appetizer made with brioche, foie gras, a grapefruit-Chardonnay sauce

Ingredients

  • 0.5 pound foie gras
  • 0.5 cup of wine
  • 1 clove of garlic
  • 0.5 tablespoon of unsalted butter
  • 0.25 tps of rosemary
  • 0.5 tps of zest orange
  • 1 tps of fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tps of salt
  • 0.5 tps of blackpepper

Steps

  1. Start preparing foie gras by splitting the two lobes and remove excess fat if there is any
  2. Carefully eliminate the veins that are visible and discard
  3. Rinse foie gras with slightly cold water
  4. Dip a sharp knife in hot water and cut foie gras in 1 inch thick slices
  5. Wrap with a cling film and put it in the refrigerator
  6. Foie gras is ready to be seasoned and cooked later
  7. Take out prepared foie gras from the refrigerator and season each slice generously with salt and freshly grounded black pepper
  8. Take a medium sized saucepan and preheat it for about 2 minutes on low flame
  9. Put slices of foie gras in the pan and sear them for about 1 minute. Be cautious; do not overcook your foie gras
  10. Turn around the slices and sear for another minute. To get a perfectly seared foie gras it should be nicely done from the outside but rare from inside
  11. Remove the slices from the pan once they are done and put aside
  12. To make wine sauce, remove any excess fat or pan drippings and put the pan back on low to medium flame
  13. Add butter, garlic, orange zest and rosemary leaves and saut√© for a few seconds
  14. Add wine and let it simmer for two minutes
  15. To enhance the flavor squeeze fresh lemon juice in it and whisk well
  16. Your wine sauce is ready
  17. To assemble, gently place foie gras slices on the serving plates and drizzle wine sauce over it
  18. Garnish with orange zest and rosemary leaves and serve straightaway
